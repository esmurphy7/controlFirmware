The controller connects to the head over XBee. When the controller receives the ASCII
character ‘j’ from the head, it must respond with this packet. If the head doesn’t
receive it within 300ms it will send another ‘j’ to retry. If it still doesn’t receive
a response it will stop the snake.

In the future we might make this controller functionality available over WiFi.


// Controller packet
// Size: 30 bytes

Byte0: Kill Switch [Boolean]
Byte1: Joystick Left [Boolean]
Byte2: Joystick Right [Boolean]
Byte3: Joystick Up [Boolean]
Byte4: Joystick Down [Boolean]
Byte5: Jaw Open [Boolean]
Byte6: Jaw Close [Boolean]
Byte7: Vertical Straighten [Boolean]
Byte8: Calibrate [Boolean]
Byte9: Vertical Straighten On The Fly [Boolean]
Byte10: Spare
Byte11: Spare
Byte12: Motor Speed [8-bit]
Byte13: Propagation Delay (10-bit] (MSB)
Byte14: Propagation Delay (10-bit]
Byte15: Spare Knob 2 (10-bit] (MSB)
Byte16: Spare Knob 2 (10-bit]
Byte17: Spare Knob 4 (10-bit] (MSB)
Byte18: Spare Knob 4 (10-bit]
Byte19: Spare
..
..
Byte29: Spare
